# Decision-maker dossier — the sales use case, pushed to its limits (2026-06-30)

The real job: a BDR wants everything public on one decision-maker + company before reaching
out. The signals live across every source type at once. This run gathers a dossier on **Matt
MacInnis (CPO, Rippling)** from 12 live public sources spanning all four fetch tiers, and
proves the two new tiers (`curl_cffi` impersonation + MarkItDown documents) carry their weight.

Reproduce: `benchmarks/decision_maker_dossier.py [--workers N]`

## What the two new tiers bought

- **`curl_cffi` impersonation tier:** WSJ returns 401 to plain `urllib` (it was in the README's
  "not supported" column). Browser-TLS impersonation gets the real article text in **670 ms** —
  no stealth browser needed. A free win for the class of sites that fingerprint TLS but don't
  serve a JS-only shell. (It does *not* crack Reddit/LinkedIn shells — those still need the
  browser; confirmed, not regressed.)
- **MarkItDown document tier:** a PDF report → **39,662 chars** of markdown. pith now ingests
  PDF/Word/PowerPoint/Excel/epub, not just HTML — decks, filings, and datasheets a sales rep
  actually encounters. Extension-routed (`.pdf` etc.); an extensionless doc URL still falls
  through to HTML handling (known limit, upgrade path = Content-Type sniff).

## Honest notes

- Glassdoor came back at 137 chars (partial wall) — counted as a hit but thin, consistent with
  the README's 🟡 rating. Everything else yielded real content.
- Walled sources dominate wall-clock (3–9 s each); the browser is the cost, exactly as the
  cross-language benchmark found. `--workers` is the lever that matters for a multi-source
  dossier, not the extraction language.
- All offline tests (19) and live canary (13/13) green with these changes.
